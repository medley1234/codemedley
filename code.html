FOOTBALL


  • The aim of the game is to score more goals than your opponent in a 90 minute playing time frame.
  • Each team consists of 11 players, including a goalkeeper, who can use any part of their body except their hands and arms to play the ball.
  • The field of play must be rectangular and marked with lines, goals, and flags.
VOLLEYBALL


  • Each team consists of 6 players and 6 substitutes.
  • Games are played up to 25 points and must be won by 2 clear points
  • Players may not hit the ball twice in succession (a block is not considered a hit)
TENNIS


  • Serving diagonally
  • Following the correct court boundaries
  • Scoring points
HOCKEY


  • Players can only hit the ball with the flat side of their stick.
  • Goals can only be scored from inside the ‘striking circle’ in front of the opponent's goal.
  • Hockey players (other than the goalkeeper) are not allowed to use their feet or any other parts of the body to control the ball.
      • ARCHREY


      • Archers must adhere to official rules regarding equipment to avoid unfair advantages.
      • Maximum time for shooting an end of three arrows is two minutes, and four minutes for an end of six arrows.
      • Scoring is based on where the arrow lands on the target
        • GYMNASTIC


        • Starting with Compulsories: Gymnasts must demonstrate their skill at several levels of compulsory routines before moving to more advanced “optional” routines.
        • Scoring: Judges score gymnasts based on their performance
        • Time Limits: Routines must be performed within specified time limits.
          • ROWING


          • The objective of rowing is to reach the finish line first
          • You can start paddling the boat after the gun fire.
          • Physical and mental strength, high levels of stamina and seamless synchronisation are required to win
